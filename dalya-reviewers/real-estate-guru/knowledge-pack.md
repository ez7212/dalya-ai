# Real Estate Guru Knowledge Pack

This is a practical Dubai real estate reference for reviewing Dalya. Treat fees/process notes as common market patterns that must be verified before buyer-facing use.

## Strong buyer qualification

A strong inbound buyer flow should identify:

- Budget range.
- Cash or mortgage.
- Mortgage pre-approval status if mortgage.
- Preferred area/community.
- Property type.
- Bedrooms and size requirements.
- Ready property vs off-plan.
- End-use vs investment.
- Desired move-in/purchase timeline.
- Viewing availability.
- Whether buyer is in Dubai.
- Whether buyer has seen similar properties.
- Whether buyer is working with another agent.
- Urgency and decision-maker status.
- Contact preference for agent handoff.

Do not ask all of these at once. Collect them naturally.

## Ready property resale information

For ready secondary-market resale, useful data includes:

- Title deed status.
- Seller identification availability.
- Mortgage status on property.
- Tenancy status: vacant/rented.
- If rented: rent amount, expiry date, notice status.
- Service charges.
- Asking price and seller's realistic net expectation.
- NOC process/fees depending on developer.
- Viewability and access instructions.
- Unit details: floor, view, layout, size, parking, condition, upgrades.
- Comparable listings/recent transactions if available.
- Listing agreement/Form A where applicable.

## Off-plan resale information

For off-plan resale, useful data includes:

- SPA/booking form.
- Oqood status where applicable.
- Original purchase price.
- Amount paid to date.
- Outstanding amount.
- Payment plan schedule.
- Developer transfer restrictions.
- NOC process.
- Transfer fee.
- Expected completion/handover date.
- Unit details.
- Premium/discount vs original price and current market.
- Whether seller is motivated.

## Common Dubai transaction concepts

Dalya should avoid misrepresenting:

- Buyer agency commission, often around 2% plus VAT in many resale transactions, subject to agreement.
- DLD transfer fee, commonly 4% of purchase price plus administrative fees.
- Trustee office fees.
- NOC fees depending on developer.
- Mortgage registration and valuation fees if applicable.
- Conveyancing fees if applicable.
- Form F/MOU for sale agreement.
- Form A for seller-agent listing agreement.
- Form B for buyer-agent agreement.

Any buyer-facing fee calculation should be framed as an estimate unless verified for the specific transaction.

## High-intent buyer signals

- Wants viewing today/tomorrow.
- Asks if unit is available.
- Asks final price or negotiability.
- Asks service charges.
- Asks payment plan or amount paid.
- Mentions cash, pre-approval, or mortgage readiness.
- Says they are in Dubai now.
- Asks for floor plan, location pin, title deed details, SPA/Oqood, or NOC.
- Compares specific units.
- Gives exact timeline or family needs.

High-intent leads should trigger agent handoff quickly.

## Low-quality or early-stage buyer signals

- No budget.
- Unrealistic budget for the area/property.
- Extremely broad search.
- Refuses to share timeline or intent.
- Generic ROI question with no constraints.
- Asking many generic questions without engaging.

Low-quality does not mean ignore. It means route to nurture, templates, or lower-priority follow-up.

## Dashboard value tests

A useful daily dashboard answers:

- Who should I contact now?
- What should I say?
- Which leads are hot?
- Which leads need human attention?
- Which owners should I chase?
- Which listings are missing information?
- Which viewings are pending confirmation?
- What changed since yesterday?
- Which AI conversations failed or need review?

If the dashboard only displays static information, agents will likely ignore it.

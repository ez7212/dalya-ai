# UX Designer Review Rubric

## 1. First-use comprehension — score 1-10

Look for:

- Can a new agent understand the product in 30 seconds?
- Is the value proposition visible from the first screen?
- Are labels in agent language, not internal product language?
- Are empty states useful?

## 2. Daily habit potential — score 1-10

Look for:

- Clear recurring workflow.
- “What changed since yesterday?”
- “What needs attention now?”
- Reasons to open Dalya before WhatsApp/CRM.

## 3. Information hierarchy — score 1-10

Look for:

- Hot leads visually prioritized.
- Urgent actions obvious.
- Low-value details hidden.
- Clear path from overview to action.
- No metric vanity cards above work queues.

## 4. Workflow friction — score 1-10

Look for:

- Too many clicks.
- Too many screens.
- Repeated data entry.
- Unclear navigation.
- Missing shortcuts.
- Too much copy/paste.
- Mobile-unfriendly flows.

## 5. Trust and explainability — score 1-10

Look for:

- AI recommendations explain why.
- Lead scores show reasons.
- Missing/uncertain data visible.
- Agent can override/correct AI.
- AI-generated copy is editable.

## 6. Onboarding and activation — score 1-10

Look for:

- Setup is short.
- First useful moment arrives quickly.
- Empty states teach by example.
- Product does not require unrealistic migration from WhatsApp.

## Required output additions

Include:

- Most confusing screen or flow.
- Biggest adoption risk.
- One change to increase daily use.
- One change to reduce cognitive load.
- Suggested dashboard homepage structure.
- Suggested empty-state copy if relevant.
- Suggested button/CTA labels if current labels are weak.

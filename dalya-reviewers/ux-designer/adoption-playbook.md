# UX Designer Adoption Playbook

## Daily-use hypothesis

Dalya becomes a habit only if it owns a recurring moment:

- Morning: “Who needs attention today?”
- Between viewings: “What should I reply?”
- After lead capture: “Is this lead hot and what is missing?”
- Before owner follow-up: “What should I send and what do I need?”
- End of day: “What did I miss?”

## Dashboard homepage should likely prioritize

1. Urgent actions.
2. Hot leads.
3. Conversations needing agent takeover.
4. Viewings pending confirmation.
5. Follow-ups due today.
6. Missing property/listing info blocking sales.
7. Recent AI activity and failures.

Metrics should not dominate unless they drive action.

## Adoption risks

- Too much setup before value.
- Too many generic cards.
- No obvious “next action.”
- Lead score shown without explanation.
- AI suggestions hidden too deep.
- Agent has to copy/paste manually into WhatsApp.
- Empty states feel dead.
- Product requires agents to abandon WhatsApp immediately.
- Dashboard is desktop-first while agent workflow is mobile/WhatsApp-heavy.

## Activation moment

A new agent should get value when they see one of these:

- A hot lead summarized with a suggested reply.
- A messy conversation turned into an actionable task.
- A follow-up they would have missed.
- A property/owner message drafted in their tone.
- A lead handoff summary good enough to send immediately.

## Useful UX patterns

- “Today” queue.
- “Needs reply” queue.
- “Hot lead” badges with reasons.
- Suggested next message with edit/copy/send.
- Clear missing-field chips.
- Inline confidence/uncertainty labels.
- One-click WhatsApp handoff/copy.
- Empty states with sample value.
- Progressive disclosure of details.
- Timeline of conversation + AI summary side-by-side.

## Bad UX patterns

- Generic dashboards full of counts.
- “AI score: 82” without reason.
- Tables with too many columns.
- Requiring agents to search/filter before seeing work.
- Requiring perfect CRM hygiene.
- Hiding the next action behind a detail page.
- Too many tabs before the first useful workflow.

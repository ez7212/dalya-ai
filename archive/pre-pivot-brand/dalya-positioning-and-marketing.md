# Dalya — Positioning & Marketing Strategy
**Prepared:** March 2026
**Based on:** Dalya Strategic Brief + Competitive analysis of 30+ AI real estate platforms across US, UK, EU, Singapore, HK, UAE

---

## Executive Summary

Every major AI real estate platform that failed had one thing in common: they built a technology narrative and attached a market to it. Every one that succeeded built a market narrative and used technology to deliver it.

Dalya's positioning must be anchored in three things competitors cannot claim:
1. **The only platform built specifically for Dubai off-plan SPA resale**
2. **Sellers: live on every portal in 30 minutes, with a real-time lead dashboard**
3. **Buyers: 1.15% total commission instead of 2%, answered in your language in seconds**

**Important Dubai commission context:** In Dubai, the buyer pays brokerage commission by default — the standard is 2% of the sale price. Sellers do not automatically pay commission unless they've signed an exclusive Form A agreement with a listing agent. This fundamentally changes how Dalya must be positioned per audience:

- **Seller pitch** is about speed, control, and visibility — not cost savings (they weren't paying commission anyway)
- **Buyer pitch** is about saving 0.85% (total drops from 2% to 1.15%: 1% to their own agent + 0.15% to Dalya as the seller's agent) — a material AED saving on a multi-million dirham transaction

The SPA-parsing capability is the moat that makes Dalya credible to investors who have been burned by generic portals and ghosting agents.

## Part 1 — The Market Context You're Entering

### What the Research Tells Us About Dubai Specifically

Off-plan transactions hit **63% of all Dubai residential sales in 2024**, up from 54% in 2023. The secondary off-plan resale market (SPAs changing hands before handover) is the fastest-growing, least-served segment in UAE property. No platform handles it end-to-end. Property Finder and Bayut know listing prices. DLD records transaction prices. Nobody has the SPA layer.

The dominant buyer nationalities — **Russian, German, British, Indian, Iranian** (confirmed from Sobha Seahaven DLD data; broadly representative of UAE off-plan market) — speak five different languages and have different negotiation styles, documentation expectations, and trust triggers. The portals surface listings in English. The agents are often English-first. This gap is real and current.

The agent trust problem in Dubai is acute. The DLD's AI Advertising Governance Platform monitored 279,000 listings and **automatically modified 29% of them for compliance failures**. Almost one in three listings is non-compliant. Sellers know this. International buyers know this. RERA-verified, DLD-registered, compliance-first positioning is not bureaucracy — it is the strongest trust signal available in this market.

### What Has Worked Globally

**Purplebricks** coined the word "commisery" — the misery of paying commission — and drove 218% revenue growth on the back of one emotional insight. They did not say "save money." They named the feeling. Then they exited the US because they failed to localize that emotion.

**Ohmyhome** in Singapore leads with "$45 million in total commission savings for customers" — a specific dollar amount, not a percentage. Their 1% commission is still 6x more expensive than Dalya's 0.15%, which means Dalya's cumulative savings story will be proportionally far more powerful once transaction volume builds.

**Redfin** described its AI search as "like talking to a real estate agent who has seen every listing in town — available 24/7." The AI is never mentioned by name. The experience is everything.

**Structurely** built a B2B AI real estate chatbot with deliberate typos and response delays to mimic human behavior. The lesson is inverted for Dalya: you should be transparent about the AI, because the AI's speed and multilingual capability are your USP — not a liability to hide.

**Rex Real Estate** attacked traditional agents as corrupt and avoided MLS entirely. Raised $80M. Shut down in 2022. The lesson: disrupting infrastructure before the market abandons it is a category error. Dalya must list on PF and Bayut, not replace them.

**The universal failure mode:** platforms that did not proactively address the "you get what you pay for" objection. Every low-commission or AI-first platform that failed left this objection unanswered. It must be answered in the same breath as the price claim.

## Part 4 — The AI Positioning Decision

### Lead with the AI, but frame it through outcomes

The research is clear on two things that appear to contradict each other:
1. Calling it a "chatbot" in consumer-facing materials consistently underperforms. It connotes scripted, limited, second-tier service.
2. Hiding the AI and having users discover they were talking to a machine generates backlash and trust destruction.

The resolution: **name the AI, disclose it honestly, and lead with what it does rather than what it is.**

### Naming Recommendation

Do not call it "chatbot," "bot," or "AI assistant." Consider a name that is regional, trustworthy, and functional.

Candidates to evaluate:
- **Dalya** — use the brand name itself as the conversational agent ("Dalya answered your inquiry")
- **Noor** — Arabic for "light"; connotes clarity and guidance
- **Raya** — works across Arabic, Russian, and English; means "flow" in some contexts
- A name that works phonetically in all five target languages without sounding awkward

The name should appear in the buyer-facing chat interface, in notification emails ("Dalya has responded to your inquiry"), and on the seller dashboard ("3 conversations handled by Dalya today").

### The Disclosure Language

Use this framing consistently:

> "You're speaking with Dalya's property advisor. It has read the complete SPA for this unit and can answer questions about payment milestones, floor plans, handover timelines, and the resale process. If you'd like to speak with a human, type 'speak to someone' at any time."

This is: transparent about AI status, specific about capabilities, and provides a clear escalation path. All three are required to maintain buyer trust.

### What the AI Should and Should Not Be Credited With

**Credit the AI for:**
- Speed (responds in seconds, 24/7)
- Knowledge (knows the SPA, the project, the payment schedule)
- Language (fluent in 5 languages, instantly)
- Consistency (never has a bad day, never misses a follow-up)

**Do not credit the AI for:**
- Valuation opinions or price recommendations
- Legal advice on SPA terms
- Investment return predictions
- Negotiation strategy

These belong to the human-in-the-loop. The AI provides information; the human provides judgment. This distinction manages liability and maintains trust.

## Part 5 — The Commission Story

### The Objection You Must Answer Before It's Asked

**The commission objection is different for each audience — address them separately.**

For **sellers**: They aren't paying commission anyway (buyers do, by Dubai convention). The objection isn't about cost — it's about service quality. *"Will a no-frills platform actually sell my unit?"* Answer: every buyer inquiry answered in seconds, in their language, 24/7. More buyers reached = better offers = faster sale. The quality proof is the response speed and the dashboard.

For **buyers**: The objection is skepticism — *"why is the seller's agent charging so little — is something wrong with the listing?"* Answer: the 0.15% is made possible by AI, not by cutting corners. The listing is RERA-compliant, DLD-verified, fully parsed from the actual SPA. The lower fee reflects efficiency, not reduced diligence.

Dalya answers the quality objection structurally: **the AI model means lower fee AND better service, simultaneously.** A 24/7 AI that responds in 5 languages in seconds is objectively superior to a human agent who responds in English in 48 hours. The lower fee is made possible by the efficiency of the AI. The quality is higher because of the same AI.

### How to Frame This in Copy

**For sellers:**
"Upload your SPA. In 30 minutes, you're live on every major portal with a valid Trakheesi permit. Every buyer who inquires — at 2am, in Russian or Mandarin — gets a real, accurate answer from your SPA within seconds. You watch every conversation from your dashboard. We handle the rest."

**For buyers:**
"Every question about this unit gets answered within 60 seconds, in your language, around the clock. The 0.15% seller commission isn't a sign of a rushed listing — it's what's possible when the process runs on AI instead of a manual agency. The listing is fully RERA-compliant and DLD-verified."

### The Incentive Alignment Signal

This is Dalya's most powerful trust signal and it is currently underused in the brief. The message:

> "We earn nothing until your unit sells. There is no scenario in which getting you a lower offer benefits us. Our only incentive is the highest possible sale price, as fast as possible."

This structural alignment is what sellers hate most about traditional agents — the suspicion that the agent wants to close fast at a lower price to free up time for the next deal. Dalya eliminates this mechanically. Say it explicitly.

| Avoid | Why | Say Instead |
|---|---|---|
| "AI-powered chatbot" | "Chatbot" = scripted, limited, second-tier | "Dalya's property advisor" |

| "Save money" as the lead | Too abstract; triggers quality skepticism | Lead with AED saved on a specific transaction |
| "Fastest growing platform" with no data | Claims without proof undermine trust | Use real numbers: listings count, days-to-sell, DLD records |

| "No agents needed" | Even if true operationally, antagonizes a channel | "Every buyer gets an instant answer. You stay in control." |

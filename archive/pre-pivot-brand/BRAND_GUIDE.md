**Version**: 1.1

**Platform**: AI-powered off-plan resale marketplace, UAE

## Section 1: Brand Strategy Foundation

### Brand Purpose
To make the UAE property market legible — giving buyers clarity and sellers reach through intelligent, honest technology.

### Brand Vision
A UAE where every property transaction, regardless of language, nationality, or experience level, begins with complete information and ends with genuine confidence.

### Brand Mission
Dalya AI connects serious buyers with verified off-plan resale listings through an intelligent, always-available AI layer that speaks clearly, negotiates fairly, and never wastes anyone's time.

### Brand Values

**1. Transparency**
Every listing price has a basis. Every AI response has a boundary. Dalya tells users what it knows and exactly when to talk to a human. In a market known for opacity, transparency is a commercial advantage.

**2. Precision**
SPA data is structured, not estimated. Offering thresholds are defined, not approximate. The brand does not use vague superlatives. This precision signals trustworthiness to both Emirati investors and expat buyers who have been burned by imprecise platforms.

**3. Accessibility**
A first-time buyer in Sharjah and an institutional investor eyeing Dubai Hills should both feel the platform was built for them. Multilingual by design, jargon-minimal by policy.

**4. Ambition**
The UAE real estate market is one of the most dynamic in the world. Dalya's technology reflects that energy — forward-thinking, AI-first, and built to scale beyond WhatsApp into a full marketplace.

### Brand Personality
Dalya's character sits at the intersection of four human archetypes:

- **The Expert Advisor**: Knowledgeable without being condescending. Can explain a payment schedule to a first-time buyer and a NOC clause to a seasoned investor.
- **The Trusted Concierge**: Available, responsive, never pushy. Does not chase buyers. Answers questions completely and steps back.
- **The Efficient Professional**: Does not pad responses. Respects the reader's time. Gets to the number quickly.
- **The Local**: Understands the UAE context — the developer ecosystem, RERA, the cultural dynamics of negotiation — without needing it explained.

### Brand Promise
Dalya will always tell you what it knows, acknowledge what it does not, and connect you to the right human when that matters. No inflation, no pressure, no confusion.

## Section 3: Visual Identity

### 3.1 Color System

#### Design Rationale — UAE Market Context

Color choices for the UAE market require navigating several intersecting signals:

- **Gold/amber** carries deep cultural resonance across Emirati, Arab, and South Asian audiences — it signals prosperity, heritage, and aspiration. Bayut and Property Finder both avoid it strategically, leaving it available for differentiation.
- **Deep teal/green** connects to Islamic cultural heritage (green is the color of paradise in Islamic tradition) and to the UAE's national identity, while reading as modern and digital in tech contexts.
- **White and near-white** backgrounds are essential for Arabic text legibility — Arabic script needs generous negative space to be readable at scale.
- **Pure black** should be avoided as a primary brand color in UAE contexts; very dark navy or charcoal reads as sophisticated without the stark coldness of pure black.

#### Primary Palette

```
Dalya Gold         #C9A96E
```
Warm gold derived from traditional Arabian jewelry and architectural gilding. Not a brash metallic gold — this is a muted, sophisticated tone that reads as premium without ostentation. Works on dark and light backgrounds. Used for: logo mark, primary CTAs, highlight accents, pricing callouts.

Cultural basis: Gold is one of the most positively charged colors across all UAE demographic groups. In Emirati culture, gold represents heritage and prosperity. In South Asian and Arab communities, it signals celebration and quality. It avoids the tourist-trap association of pure #FFD700 by being desaturated and warm.

```
Dalya Ink          #1A2B3C
```
A deep navy — not pure black. Conveys authority, precision, and trust. Used for: primary text, dark backgrounds, header elements, data tables. The blue undertone keeps it from reading as harsh and connects subliminally to digital trust signals (finance, technology).

```
Dalya Slate        #2E4057
```
Mid-range navy, lighter than Ink. Used for: secondary text, card backgrounds in dark mode, navigation elements, subtle section dividers.

#### Secondary Palette

```
Dalya Sage         #4A7C6F
```
A muted teal-green. Connects to Islamic green heritage while reading as modern and growth-oriented. Distinct from the greens used by Property Finder (bright #00C814) and Bayut (orange/blue). Used for: success states, verified badges, trust indicators, secondary CTAs.

```
Dalya Sand         #F5EFE6
```
Warm off-white inspired by UAE desert sand and traditional Arabic architecture (limestone, travertine). Used for: page backgrounds, card surfaces, light mode UI. Avoids the clinical coldness of pure white.

```
Dalya Copper       #B87333
```
A deeper, warmer version of the gold palette. Used for: hover states on Gold elements, premium tier indicators, borders on featured listings. Creates depth in the gold system without adding new colors.

#### Neutral Palette

```
Neutral 50         #FAFAF8    (warm white, near-background)
Neutral 100        #F5EFE6    (= Dalya Sand — light surfaces)
Neutral 200        #E8E0D5    (dividers, borders)
Neutral 300        #C9BFB3    (placeholder text, disabled states)
Neutral 500        #8A8078    (secondary text)
Neutral 700        #4A4540    (body text on light)
Neutral 900        #1A2B3C    (= Dalya Ink — primary text)
```

#### Semantic Colors

```
Success            #4A7C6F    (= Dalya Sage)
Warning            #E8A045    (amber, distinct from Gold)
Error              #C0392B    (accessible red)
Info               #2980B9    (clear blue, neutral)
```

#### Accessibility Notes
- Dalya Gold (#C9A96E) on Dalya Ink (#1A2B3C): contrast ratio 6.8:1 — passes WCAG AA for all text sizes
- Dalya Ink (#1A2B3C) on Dalya Sand (#F5EFE6): contrast ratio 12.4:1 — passes WCAG AAA
- Dalya Sage (#4A7C6F) on Neutral 50 (#FAFAF8): contrast ratio 4.6:1 — passes WCAG AA for normal text
- Warning amber (#E8A045) must only be used on Ink backgrounds for accessible text

#### Dark Mode Mapping

| Light Mode Role | Dark Mode Value |
|---|---|
| Page background | #0F1923 (deeper than Ink) |
| Card surface | #1A2B3C (= Dalya Ink) |
| Secondary surface | #2E4057 (= Dalya Slate) |

### 3.2 Logo Direction

The Dalya wordmark should be built on three design principles:

**Geometric clarity**: Clean, modern letterforms that work at 16px favicon scale and 600px header scale equally. No scripts or calligraphic styles — these create Arabic/English inconsistency problems.

**The mark**: A stylized letter D that incorporates a subtle arc suggesting both a door (Arabic: باب — threshold, entry point) and a location pin. The arc in the letterform should use Dalya Gold against the Ink background or reverse. The concept of a threshold is culturally resonant — Arabic real estate culture frames property purchase as entering a new chapter of life.

**Wordmark**: Set in the primary typeface (see Section 4) using the Ink color on light backgrounds and Sand on dark backgrounds. The AI designation should appear small beneath the wordmark in Sage — not bolted to the logo as "Dalya AI" but as a quiet descriptor that professionals will notice.

**Logo Variants Required**:
- Full horizontal: mark + wordmark + "AI" descriptor
- Compact horizontal: mark + wordmark only
- Icon only: the D-arc mark in a square or circle container
- Reverse (for dark backgrounds): Sand/Gold on transparent
- Single color: for embossing, watermarks, PDF headers

**Clear Space**: Minimum clear space equal to the height of the lowercase "a" in the wordmark on all sides.

**Minimum Size**: Icon variant minimum 24px. Full horizontal wordmark minimum 120px width.

### 3.3 Iconography

Icon style: **Outlined with 1.5px stroke weight**, rounded terminals, 24px grid. Avoid filled icons except for active/selected states. This style reads as modern and professional without looking like a generic Material Icons clone.

Key icon set required:
- Property types (apartment, villa, townhouse, penthouse)
- UAE-specific: off-plan, under construction, handover stages
- Financial: payment schedule, NOC status, AED currency
- Trust signals: verified, RERA registered, AI-analyzed
- Communication: WhatsApp (use brand color, not green), escalation, human agent

Avoid: Generic globe icons, stock real estate imagery of keys and handshakes. These read as low-trust in the UAE market.

### 3.4 Photography and Imagery Direction

**Architecture photography**: Clean, natural light, minimal people. The UAE market responds to architectural beauty — let buildings speak. Avoid drone clichés (top-down shots of Dubai Marina at sunset are overexposed in this market).

**Lifestyle photography**: If people appear, ensure demographic representation reflects the actual UAE buyer mix — not only Western or only Emirati. A mix of South Asian, Arab, and Western profiles in professional contexts.

**Avoid**:
- Stock photography of families with keys
- Nighttime golden-hour Dubai skyline shots (overused by every competitor)
- Images that look like they are from a generic North American real estate platform

**Data visualization**: Property data, payment schedules, price comparisons. These should use the Dalya color system — Gold for primary data series, Sage for secondary, Sand for background. Tables and charts are trust signals in this market; invest in making them beautiful.

## Section 4: Typography

### 4.1 The Bilingual Challenge

The UAE market requires genuine Arabic-English bilingual design, not an afterthought Arabic font. This has direct implications for font selection:

- Arabic and Latin fonts must share optical weight and x-height compatibility
- Line height requirements differ: Arabic needs more vertical space (typically 1.8x vs 1.5x for Latin)
- Arabic is right-to-left; layout systems must be RTL-ready from architecture, not retrofitted

### 4.2 Recommended Type System

**Primary Typeface (Headlines, UI Labels): Plus Jakarta Sans**
- Open source (SIL), excellent for digital interfaces
- Geometric-humanist hybrid: authoritative but approachable
- Very good Latin character coverage
- Pairs well with Arabic companions due to geometric proportions
- Weights to license: Light (300), Regular (400), Medium (500), SemiBold (600), Bold (700)

**Arabic Companion: IBM Plex Arabic**
- Designed to pair with IBM Plex Sans (similar geometric-humanist DNA to Plus Jakarta Sans)
- Open source (SIL)
- Optimized for screen rendering
- Covers full Arabic script including extended character sets needed for UAE context
- Works at display and body sizes without separate display/text cuts

**Alternative primary if Plus Jakarta Sans is not available: Inter**
- Universal availability, excellent legibility at small sizes
- Arabic pair: IBM Plex Arabic (same recommendation)

**Body Text (Long-form content, property descriptions): Source Serif 4**
- Provides editorial warmth for longer reads (investment rationales, area guides)
- Variable font — one file covers all weights
- Arabic equivalent for body: Noto Serif Arabic

**Monospace (Data, SPA numbers, unit references): JetBrains Mono**
- For displaying AED figures, unit numbers, payment percentages in data contexts
- Conveys precision and technical integrity
- No Arabic equivalent needed — numbers in property data are universally displayed in Western numerals in the UAE market

### 4.3 Type Scale

Based on a 16px base with a 1.25 (Major Third) modular scale:

```
Display XL:   48px / 56px line-height / SemiBold    — Hero headlines
Display L:    38px / 46px line-height / SemiBold    — Section titles
Heading 1:    30px / 38px line-height / Bold         — Page titles
Heading 2:    24px / 32px line-height / SemiBold    — Card titles, subsections
Heading 3:    20px / 28px line-height / Medium       — Labels, groupings
Body Large:   18px / 28px line-height / Regular     — Lead paragraph text
Body:         16px / 24px line-height / Regular     — Default body
Body Small:   14px / 22px line-height / Regular     — Captions, secondary info
Label:        12px / 16px line-height / Medium       — Tags, badges, metadata
Micro:        10px / 14px line-height / Medium       — Legal, footnotes
```

Arabic text should increase line-height by approximately 20% at each step.

### 4.4 Typography Rules

- Never use Plus Jakarta Sans Bold below 14px — use Medium instead
- AED values should always use JetBrains Mono to signal precision
- Percentage figures (payment complete, price change) use JetBrains Mono in Heading 3 weight
- Arabic numerals in body text should be Eastern Arabic (٠١٢٣٤٥٦٧٨٩) when the UI is in Arabic mode; switch to Western Arabic numerals in English mode
- Never mix typefaces within a single UI component

## Section 5: Brand Voice

### 5.1 Voice Characteristics

**Direct**
Dalya says the price. It does not "invite you to inquire." It does not "offer competitive rates." It states: AED 2,100,000 asking. 20% paid. NOC eligible at 40%. This directness is rare in UAE property marketing and will be remembered.

**Knowledgeable without condescension**
The AI knows what a NOC is, what RERA means, how a payment schedule works. It explains these things when relevant, without being asked and without being pedantic. It calibrates to the sophistication of the question.

**Warm but not overfamiliar**
The UAE professional market values courtesy. Dalya is never cold or transactional, but it does not pretend to be a friend. "This listing matches your budget" is warmer than "listing found." "We can arrange that" is colder than "Dalya can organize a call for you."

**Boundary-honest**
When Dalya does not know something, it says so and says what happens next. "That question is with the seller — you'll hear back within 24 hours." No hallucinated confidence.

**Culturally fluent**
Dalya understands that negotiation is expected. It does not treat a below-asking offer as offensive. It processes it according to threshold logic and responds with grace. When someone writes in Arabic, it responds in Arabic — not a transliterated English response.

### 5.2 Tone Variations

**For buyers making first contact**: Warm, informative, efficient. Answer the actual question fast, then provide one level of additional context they probably need but didn't ask for.

**For buyers making offers**: Neutral, precise. No emotive language about "exciting opportunities." State what was received, state what the threshold is (if above), state what happens next.

**For escalation alerts (to Eric/sellers)**: Clinical, data-first. Buyer name, property reference, offer amount, timestamp. What they need to decide and how to respond.

**For seller onboarding**: Professional, process-oriented. Clear steps, clear expectations, no overselling.

**In Arabic**: More formal than the English equivalent. Arabic business communication in the UAE, particularly in real estate, carries higher formality expectations. The AI should default to formal Modern Standard Arabic (فصحى) for new contacts and follow the user's register if they shift to Gulf dialect.

### 5.3 Messaging Architecture

**Brand Tagline**
"Property intelligence. No pressure."

Rationale: Addresses two of the biggest purchase barriers in the UAE market — lack of reliable information and high-pressure broker tactics. Both words ("intelligence" and "pressure") carry double meaning that works in context. This is the strategic brand-level line — used in brand identity, platform positioning, and investor communications.

**Product Line / Campaign Tagline**
"Your SPA. Live on every portal. Sold."

Rationale: A tactical, campaign-level expression tied directly to the product experience. Encodes the three-step seller value proposition (upload SPA → live on portals → sell) in seven words. Ownable and specific to Dalya's off-plan resale product. Used in performance marketing, landing pages, and seller-facing copy. Every piece of external communication should be traceable back to this line.

The two taglines serve distinct roles and should never compete on the same surface. The brand tagline leads brand-level contexts; the product line leads product and campaign contexts.

Secondary tagline option for Arabic-first contexts:
"عقارات بلا ضغوط — ذكاء حقيقي"
("Real estate without pressure — genuine intelligence")

Arabic campaign line (product-level, seller-facing):
"وثيقتك. على كل البوابات. مباعة."
("Your SPA. On every portal. Sold.")

**Value Proposition**
Dalya turns your SPA into a live, intelligent listing — answering buyer questions instantly, qualifying serious leads, and escalating to you only when it matters.

**Key Messages by Audience**

For sellers:
1. "Your off-plan unit. Live on every portal. In 30 minutes."
2. "Upload your SPA. Go live in 30 minutes. Watch every inquiry from your dashboard."
3. "Non-exclusive. RERA-compliant. Every buyer answered in their language."

For buyers:
1. "Ask anything. Get answered instantly. In your language."
2. "Pay 1.15% total commission — not the market standard 2%."
3. "Every question about the SPA answered within 60 seconds. 24 hours a day."

**Trust line** (used across both audiences):
"RERA-registered. DLD-verified. Every listing sourced from the actual SPA."

**Incentive alignment line** (for sellers — currently underused; deploy explicitly in seller onboarding, FAQs, and landing pages):
"We earn nothing until your unit sells. There is no scenario in which getting you a lower offer benefits us."

**Vocabulary Guidance**

Use: property, listing, unit, SPA, asking price, payment schedule, handover, developer, NOC, RERA, AED, off-plan resale
Avoid: home (too American), deal (sounds transactional/pressured), investment opportunity (overused and signals hype), luxury (use only when specifically true), exclusive (unless actually exclusive)

**Language to avoid — full reference table**:

| Avoid | Why | Say Instead |

| "AI-powered chatbot" | "Chatbot" = scripted, limited, second-tier | "Dalya's property advisor" |

| "Your dream home" | Too American, too personal | Use "unit," "property," "listing" |
| "Seamless experience" | Cliché, says nothing | Describe the specific experience |
| "Cutting-edge AI" | Tech hype | "Dalya's property advisor knows your SPA" |

### 5.4 AI Naming and Disclosure Standards

**How to refer to the AI**

Never use: "chatbot," "bot," "AI assistant"
Always use: "Dalya's property advisor"

This language applies in all product UI, marketing copy, seller communications, buyer-facing chat interfaces, notification emails, and support documentation. "Dalya's property advisor" positions the AI as a capable, knowledgeable professional rather than a scripted automation. This framing is backed by research showing that "chatbot" language consistently underperforms in consumer trust contexts.

**Required disclosure language**

Use this verbatim in product UI at the start of any buyer conversation:

"You're speaking with Dalya's property advisor. It has read the complete SPA for this unit and can answer questions about payment milestones, floor plans, handover timelines, and the resale process. If you'd like to speak with a human, type 'speak to someone' at any time."

This disclosure is: transparent about AI status, specific about capabilities, and provides a clear human escalation path. All three elements are required — do not shorten or paraphrase.

**What the AI should be credited for**

- Speed: responds in seconds, 24 hours a day
- Knowledge: knows the SPA, the project, the payment schedule
- Language: fluent in English, Arabic, Russian, Mandarin, and Hindi, instantly
- Consistency: never has a bad day, never misses a follow-up

**What the AI must NOT be credited for**

- Valuation opinions or price recommendations
- Legal advice on SPA terms
- Investment return predictions
- Negotiation strategy

These belong to the human-in-the-loop. The AI provides information; the human provides judgment. This distinction manages liability and maintains buyer trust. In copy and UI, never imply the AI makes decisions — it answers questions and escalates when judgment is required.

## Section 6: Visual Style and Design Language

### 6.1 Design Principles

**Data as design**
Payment schedules, price histories, unit specifications — these are content, not supporting content. Design them with the same visual care given to hero images. A beautiful, legible data table builds more trust in UAE property buyers than a lifestyle photograph.

**Negative space is respect**
Arabic script is dense. Property data is dense. The platform must breathe. Generous padding, clear visual hierarchy, and minimal decorative elements are not minimalism for its own sake — they are a usability requirement for bilingual content.

**Status is always visible**
A UAE buyer needs to know immediately: Is this listing active? Is the price current? Is the NOC threshold met? These signals must be surfaced in card design as first-class visual elements, not buried in property detail pages.

**Gold is a signal, not a decoration**
Use Dalya Gold purposefully: CTAs, pricing, trust badges, verified status. Every time a user sees gold, it should mean something. If it appears on section dividers and background accents, its signal value collapses.

### 6.2 Layout Direction

**Grid**: 12-column for desktop, 4-column for mobile, 8-column for tablet. All layouts must work in RTL without redesign — use logical CSS properties (padding-inline-start, not padding-left).

**Card anatomy** for property listings:
- Property image (16:9 ratio) or render placeholder
- Developer badge (top-left, Sage background)
- Verified SPA badge (top-right, Gold on Ink)
- Property title and unit reference
- Price in JetBrains Mono, XL weight, Gold
- Payment complete % as a visual progress indicator
- Key stats row: BUA, Plot, Bedrooms
- NOC status pill (Sage = eligible, Neutral = not yet, Copper = approaching threshold)
- CTA: "Ask Dalya" (WhatsApp deeplink or web chat)

**Navigation**: Minimal. Primary nav maximum 5 items. No mega-menus. The platform's intelligence should reduce the need for navigation — users should be able to ask Dalya rather than browse menus.

**Buyer commission savings — display rule**: Savings are always expressed in AED at specific property values, never as a percentage alone. Percentages without an AED anchor are too abstract and trigger quality skepticism. Use the savings table format at minimum three price points:

| Property value | Standard commission (2%) | With Dalya (1.15%) | Buyer saves |
|---|---|---|---|
| AED 1,500,000 | AED 30,000 | AED 17,250 | AED 12,750 |
| AED 2,000,000 | AED 40,000 | AED 23,000 | AED 17,000 |
| AED 3,500,000 | AED 70,000 | AED 40,250 | AED 29,750 |

*Buyer pays 1% to their own agent + 0.15% to Dalya as seller's agent. Standard market rate is 2% total.*

Apply this table format (or a condensed version of it) wherever buyer savings are referenced in product UI, landing pages, and marketing materials. A specific AED figure on a named property value is the highest-converting way to communicate this proposition.

### 6.3 Motion and Interaction

Keep animation purposeful and minimal. The UAE B2B-adjacent buyer demographic is on mobile, often reviewing listings during work hours. Heavy animations are distracting.

Appropriate motion:
- Subtle fade-in on card load (150ms ease-out)
- Progress bar animation on payment schedule (400ms ease-in-out on scroll into view)
- Typing indicator in chat interface (matches WhatsApp behavior — familiar to UAE users)
- Gentle pulse on "new listing" badge (not a strobe — a slow, 3s opacity pulse)

Avoid: Scroll-jacking, parallax, auto-playing video, spinning loaders (use skeleton screens instead).

### 6.4 Illustration Style

If illustrations are used (onboarding flows, empty states, error pages), the recommended approach is **geometric line art** using the brand palette:

- Isometric architectural forms derived from UAE building profiles (not the Burj Khalifa — use more abstract geometric forms that suggest UAE architecture without being a cliché)
- No people in illustrations — this avoids the cultural complexity of representing demographic mix in stylized form
- Gold and Sage as primary illustration colors, Ink for structure, Sand for backgrounds

## Section 7: Brand Protection

### 7.1 Trademark Strategy

**Priority registrations**:
- "Dalya AI" wordmark — UAE (MOCCAE/TIPO)
- "Dalya AI" wordmark — GCC region bloc registration
- "دالية" Arabic transliteration — UAE registration
- Logo mark (the D-arc device) — UAE design patent

Note on "Dalya": دالية (Dāliya) is an Arabic word meaning a climbing vine or grapevine — a metaphor for organic growth, connection, and abundance. This meaning should inform brand storytelling. It is not currently trademarked in UAE real estate contexts (verify via current TIPO search before filing).

**Domain protection**:
- dalya.ai (primary, preferred)
- dalya.ae
- dalyaai.com
- getdalya.com (acquisition/redirect fallback)

### 7.2 Brand Compliance Requirements

Any team member, developer, or partner using Dalya brand assets must adhere to:

- The AI tag must never be removed from the Dalya wordmark in external communications — "Dalya" without "AI" creates confusion with the Arabic common word usage
- Dalya Gold must not be reproduced in CMYK without a brand-approved color profile — the warm tone shifts significantly under standard CMYK conversion
- The brand must not be co-branded with individual property developers without explicit approval — association with a single developer undermines the platform's neutral marketplace positioning
- WhatsApp business profile must use the brand mark, not a property image or developer logo

### 7.3 Brand Monitoring

**Digital monitoring priorities**:
- UAE property forums (Dubizzle community, Bayut forum, Reddit r/dubai, r/abudhabi) for brand mentions
- Google Alerts for "Dalya AI real estate UAE"
- MOCCAE trademark watch for conflicting filings
- App store monitoring for clone or confusion applications

**Quarterly brand audit**:
- Review all active WhatsApp bot interactions for tone consistency against brand voice guidelines
- Audit Property Finder and Bayut listing templates for visual compliance
- Review any seller-generated content (descriptions, uploaded images) for brand alignment before publication

**CTA note**: CTAs must always describe a specific action tied to the product, never generic onboarding language. "Upload your SPA" signals that Dalya understands the seller's asset. "Ask Dalya" signals immediate utility to the buyer. Generic alternatives ("Get started," "Learn more," "Sign up") have been shown to underperform in this context and are not permitted on seller- or buyer-facing primary CTAs.

### 8.3 What to Build First

For the immediate seller dashboard and chatbot frontend (Phase 2 of the roadmap), the minimum viable brand implementation is:

1. Design tokens as CSS custom properties (Section 8.1 above)
2. Plus Jakarta Sans loaded via Google Fonts (free, fast CDN)
3. IBM Plex Arabic loaded via Google Fonts for Arabic UI strings
4. JetBrains Mono for AED figures and unit references
5. The listing card component (defined in Section 6.2) as the primary UI primitive
6. WhatsApp business profile updated with brand icon mark

The full logo design should be commissioned before any public-facing launch. The CSS token system can be built immediately with a wordmark placeholder.

## Appendix B: Cultural Considerations Checklist

Before any public brand activation, confirm:

- [ ] All marketing copy reviewed by a native Gulf Arabic speaker for tone and register appropriateness
- [ ] Property images do not show mixed-gender social scenarios that could be culturally sensitive
- [ ] No imagery of alcohol, gambling, or religiously sensitive content in any composite scenes
- [ ] Ramadan communications plan prepared (tone shifts to more reflective, community-oriented messaging during Ramadan; promotional urgency language is inappropriate)
- [ ] Eid communication templates prepared (genuine celebration, not a sales pitch)
- [ ] Arabic version of all legal/compliance copy reviewed by a UAE-qualified translator (not Google Translate)
- [ ] Brand colors tested on UAE-standard screen calibrations (displays in this market often have higher brightness and saturation defaults)

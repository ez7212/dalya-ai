# Arabic Demand + Supply Research — Dubai Off-Plan Resale (Seller-Side)

**Researcher:** arabic-researcher
**Date:** 2026-04-24
**Scope:** Arabic-language (العربية) demand signals + supply audit for Dalya off-plan resale content strategy
**Methodology:** Public-web searches in Arabic (MSA + Khaleeji dialect), Google.ae Arabic surface, publisher audit. Qualitative only.

---

## 1. Summary — Key Arabic Findings

1. **Arabic SEO supply is fragmented and seller-thin.** Large portals (Bayut, PropertyFinder, Dubizzle) all have Arabic subdirectories, but the best-ranking Arabic seller-focused content is on **Bayut's mybayut/ar** blog (`/%D8%A7%D8%B9%D8%A7%D8%AF%D8%A9-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%A8%D9%8A%D8%B9-%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%AE%D8%B1%D9%8A%D8%B7%D8%A9/`). Beyond that, the Arabic SERP is dominated by small boutique brokerages (dxbproperties, excelproperties, luxurysignature, westgatedubai, ghafwoods) and Arabic law firms (hhslawyers, emirates.jurists, barrister.ae, advocator, atblegal, ilaw.ae). These are thin, SEO-driven, and frequently machine-translated.
2. **English-only gap among top developers.** **Emaar, Sobha, Damac, and Nakheel all publish their detailed resale/NOC/SPA procedures almost exclusively in English** on `.com` domains. Emaar's Arabic presence is marketing pages only; Sobha Realty has an Arabic sub-site but no seller/resale guides; Nakheel's Arabic content is sparse. Developer-specific Arabic resale guidance is a complete void — Dalya's biggest opening.
3. **Regulatory authorities publish in Arabic — but only raw service pages, not guides.** Dubai Land Department (dubailand.gov.ae) has full Arabic service catalogs for Trakheesi, Oqood, SPA registration, NOC, Power of Attorney, DLD valuation — but the language is bureaucratic/procedural, never educational. **No Arabic government publisher explains the seller's full off-plan resale journey end-to-end.**
4. **Arabic press covers the macro, not the mechanic.** Al Bayan, Emarat Al Youm, Al Khaleej, Sky News Arabia, Asharq Business, Arabian Business, CNN Business Arabic, Al Jazeera, Al Arabiya all publish Arabic real estate news (quarterly transaction totals, top nationalities, tokenization launches). **Transaction-mechanics coverage is almost absent** — the one exception is an Emarat Al Youm piece (2024-09-22) warning that off-plan resale is "quick profit or unguaranteed speculation."
5. **Dialect matters.** The content that exists is uniformly MSA (Modern Standard Arabic / فصحى). Khaleeji (Gulf) dialect queries (e.g., "شلون أبيع", "وش رأيكم") return zero relevant results — the Arabic SERP is structurally misaligned with how actual Emirati / GCC sellers phrase their questions in informal search and voice.
6. **Seller persona content is nearly non-existent in Arabic.** The Arabic web has buyer guides, investment guides, and general market reports. Arabic **seller-side** content — "how do I exit my off-plan", "what NOC fee will Emaar charge me", "can I sell from abroad", "can I avoid the 2% broker commission" — is thin to empty.
7. **Dalya's licence story (RERA + Mahoroba + 0.15%) has no Arabic analogue** in the SERP. No Arabic publisher frames a budget brokerage as a regulated, licensed alternative to the 2% industry standard.

## 6. RTL / Script / Dialect Notes Relevant to Content Strategy

1. **Dialect register.** Publish canonical long-form in **MSA (فصحى)** — it's what the top-ranking Arabic pages (Bayut, Al Bayan, DLD) use and what SEO rewards. Use **Khaleeji/Emirati dialect sparingly**, only in:
   - Voice-of-customer case studies and testimonials.
   - Short-form social (Twitter/X, Instagram captions, TikTok).
   - WhatsApp / advisor chat copy (buyer trigger is "feels like a real Dubai person", not "formal news anchor").
   - Do **not** publish long-form articles exclusively in dialect — it hurts both SEO and perceived authority.

2. **RTL typography.** The project tokens (Plus Jakarta Sans for UI, JetBrains Mono for AED figures) must be paired with an Arabic-script family that sits next to them. Recommended pairings:
   - UI: **"IBM Plex Arabic"** or **"Rubik"** (both have matching geometric modern Arabic cuts to sit next to Plus Jakarta Sans).
   - Data/Mono: **"IBM Plex Sans Arabic"** medium weight for AED figures written in Arabic-Indic numerals where contextually appropriate. Default to **Western Arabic numerals (0-9)** for AED amounts — that's the UAE market norm; even Arabic newspapers use them.

3. **Logical CSS.** As CLAUDE.md flags: use `margin-inline-start` / `padding-inline-end` / `inset-inline` now. Avoid `margin-left/right`. Mirror icons that carry directionality (back arrows, chevrons) via `transform: scaleX(-1)` or explicit Arabic variants.

4. **Numeral handling.** Always display AED amounts with Western digits + Arabic thousand separators: `1,250,000 د.إ` not `١٬٢٥٠٬٠٠٠ درهم`. This matches how DLD, Al Bayan and Emarat Al Youm render figures.

5. **Tagline translation.** "Property intelligence. No pressure." renders as **"ذكاء عقاري. بلا ضغط."** Short, matches Dalya's precise/inviting register, and the rhyme (ذكاء / ضغط via the assonant ا / ـَ) carries the brand's restraint.

6. **CTA translation (do not deviate — same rule as English CTAs):**
   - Seller: **"ارفع عقد البيع والشراء"** (Upload your SPA) — NOT "ابدأ الآن" / "سجّل عقارك".
   - Buyer: **"اسأل داليا"** (Ask Dalya) — NOT "استفسر" / "تواصل معنا".
   - AI naming: **"مستشار داليا العقاري"** (Dalya's property advisor) — NEVER "روبوت" / "مساعد ذكي".

7. **Brand-name transliteration.** "Dalya" → **داليا** (not دلية, not دالية). The alif-yaa-alif pattern reads as a feminine name in Gulf Arabic and keeps phonetic parity across MSA and Khaleeji. Verify once with a native-speaker reviewer before shipping.

8. **URL slugs.** Use **English/Latin slugs** with Arabic `<title>` and `<h1>`. Arabic URL slugs (percent-encoded) create 300-char+ URLs that fragment in WhatsApp/Twitter share contexts. This matches what PropertyFinder, Bayut English, and DLD's English side do — and contradicts what Bayut's mybayut/ar does (which produces extremely long URLs that get truncated in shares). This is a concrete UX win Dalya can claim.

9. **hreflang.** Every canonical English page should have `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar-AE" href=".../ar/..." />` and `x-default` pointing at the English version. Dubai-market Arabic searchers use a mix of `ar-AE`, `ar-SA`, `ar`, and `ar-x-gulf` in browser headers; `ar-AE` is the right primary code, with `ar` as fallback.

10. **Bi-directional inline content.** When an AED figure or English developer name appears inside Arabic prose, wrap with `<span dir="ltr">` to avoid ligature-break artefacts — especially for strings like `AED 1,250,000` or `Emaar Beachfront`.
